  • Application for Umrah Visa will not be considered if following requirements are not met

    1. The applicant must have a passport with a validity of at least six (6) months and acceptable both for entry to Saudi Arabia and entry to the next destination. The passport should have at least six empty visa pages adjacent to eact other.

    2. Each applicant must submit 4 recent 4 x 6 size color photograph with a white background. The photograph must be a full-face view Hijab (if women) in which the visa applicant is facing the camera directly. Side or angled views are NOT accepted.

    3. The Mahram should give his complete information to travel agency of this spouse and children or any relative with whom he is travelling. He should also provide copies of supportive documents such as marriage of birth certificates.

    4. Male applicats under the legal age of (21) will not be granted an Umrah visa if not accompanied by their family.

    5. ALL women are required to travel for Umrah with a Mahram. Proof of kinship must be submitted. Women over the age of fourty-five (45) may travel without a Mahram with an organized group, They must, however submits a no objection letter form her husband, son or brother authorizing her to travel for Umrah with the named group (sample of Consent letter will be given by travel agency).

    6. The applicant must submit proof of vaccination for meningitis and ACYW135. For infants and children up to fifteen (15) years of age, a vaccination report is required for polio as well as meningitis and polio. Children over fifteen (15) years of age should present the same vaccinations requested for adults.

    7. Umrah visas will only be granted once the competent authorities in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia have   approved the applicant's request.

    8. The validity of the Umrah visa is 1 month. The length of stay in the Kingdom will depend on the duration of the Umrah program chosen by the applicant.

    9. If the applicant has converted to Islam, he/she should provide an Islamic certificate notarized by an Islamic Center.
